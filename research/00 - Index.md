## Key Themes Across This Research

### The Transformation Imperative

Indonesia's ambition to simultaneously become a financial centre, manufacturing hub, and corporate centre is historically unprecedented. The research shows that 70-88% of transformations fail, and past success is a poor predictor of future performance in discontinuous change. The 1,000 SOE leaders must navigate multiple simultaneous transformations while managing political complexity, hybrid workforces, and generational shifts.

### The Western Framework Limitation

Established competency frameworks (Korn Ferry, DDI, etc.) were built from American Fortune 500 executives and encode Western cultural assumptions about leadership. They systematically undervalue capabilities that matter in high power distance, collectivist contexts. Direct application to Indonesian SOEs will produce misleading results.

### The AI Capability Discontinuity

AI fluency is no longer optional—it's a gating criterion for 2030+ leadership. But "AI fluency" means more than using ChatGPT. Leaders must understand different AI paradigms (Anthropic vs. OpenAI), agent orchestration, hybrid workforce management, and AI governance. The capability frontier is more advanced than public models suggest.

### Assessment Must Look Forward

Traditional assessment focuses on track record—what leaders have accomplished. For transformation readiness, we must assess trajectory and potential: learning agility, cognitive flexibility, tolerance for ambiguity, self-awareness accuracy. These predict transformation success better than past results.
